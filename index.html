Ratan Naval Tata (D.O.B :28 December 1937)

Ratan Tata is actively involved in philanthropy through initiatives like the Tata Trusts, focusing on healthcare, education, and rural development. His charitable contributions aim to make a positive impact on society, addressing various social and economic challenges.

ABOUT

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist and the formar chairman of Tata sons, the hliding company of Tata Group. He led the group for over two decades, transporming it into a global business powerhouse.

Ratan Tata's achievements span several decades, and here are some notable milestones in his career:

  1. 1991: Becomes the Chairman of Tata Sons.
  2. 1998: Launches the Tata Indica, India's first indigenously developed passenger car.
  3. 2000: Oversees the acquisition of Tetley by Tata Tea, marking the company's entry into the global beverage market.
  4. 2001: Guides Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) to become the first Indian software services company to achieve annual revenues of $1 billion.
  5. 2007: The Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car, is launched under his leadership.
  6. 2008: Acquires British steelmaker Corus, making Tata Steel one of the world's top steel producers.
  7. 2012: Steps down as the Chairman of Tata Sons, passing the baton to Cyrus Mistry, and becomes Chairman Emeritus.
  8. 2016: Tata Group acquires the steel business of the U.K.'s largest steelmaker, Port Talbot, securing jobs and stabilizing the industry.
  9. 2020: Tata Sons removes Cyrus Mistry as Chairman, and Ratan Tata takes over as interim Chairman amid corporate leadership changes.
  10. Throughout his tenure, Ratan Tata has not only led the Tata Group to unprecedented heights but has also been recognized for his commitment to corporate social responsibility and philanthropy.
